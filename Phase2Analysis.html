Results of Phases 1 & 2 (SONA vs. Mturk)

We collected 56 participants from the WSU SONA system and 52 participants using Amazon's Mechanical Turk to replicate the Vanderveldt findings. Two separate recruitment strategies were used to prepare for future phases of this dissertation that will use Mechanical Turk. Vanderveldt et al. used their university recruitment system so we wanted to compare our SONA system to Mturk before beginning the remaining phases.

Hypothetical Deal Results

Below you will find results for both prospects (i.e., $40k & $800). Note the expected hyperboloid trend for all prospects and collection methods, and that delay impacts the $800 prospect far more than $40k prospect. Therefore, delaying an outcome has a larger effect on subjective value as you deal with smaller prospects. The results for Mturk appear to fit the model better than SONA.
